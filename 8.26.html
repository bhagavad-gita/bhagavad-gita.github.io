8.26 \xe2\x80\x9cukla-k\xe2\x80\x9e\xc5\x92\xe2\x80\x9ae gat\xe2\x80\x9d hy etejagata\xc5\xa1 \xe2\x80\x9c\xe2\x88\x92\xe2\x80\x9cvate mateekay\xe2\x88\x92 y\xe2\x88\x92ty an\xe2\x88\x92v\xe2\x80\x9ettimanyay\xe2\x88\x92vartate puna\xc5\xa1\xe2\x80\x9cukla\xc3\x91light; k\xe2\x80\x9e\xc5\x92\xe2\x80\x9ae\xc3\x91darkness; gat\xe2\x80\x9d\xc3\x91passing away; hi\xc3\x91certainly; ete\xc3\x91all these; jagata\xc5\xa1\xc3\x91of the material world; \xe2\x80\x9c\xe2\x88\x92\xe2\x80\x9cvate\xc3\x91the Vedas; mate\xc3\x91in the opinion; ekay\xe2\x88\x92\xc3\x91by one; y\xe2\x88\x92ti\xc3\x91goes; an\xe2\x88\x92v\xe2\x80\x9ettim\xc3\x91no return; anyay\xe2\x88\x92\xc3\x91by the other; \xe2\x88\x92vartate\xc3\x91comes back; puna\xc5\xa1\xc3\x91again. According to the Vedas, there are two ways of passing from this world\xc3\x91one in light and one in darkness. When one passes in light, he does not come back; but when one passes in darkness, he returns.
