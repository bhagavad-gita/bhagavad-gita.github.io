4 mekhalajina-vasamsi jata-danda-kamandalun bibhryad upavitam ca darbha-panir yathoditam SYNONYMS mekhala--a belt made of straw; ajina-vasamsi--garments made of deerskin; jata--matted hair; danda--a rod; kamandalun--and a waterpot known as a kamandalu; bibhryat--he (the brahmacari) should regularly carry or wear; upavitam ca--and a sacred thread; darbha-panih--taking purified kusa in his hand; yatha uditam--as recommended in the sastras. TRANSLATION Carrying pure kusa grass in his hand, the brahmacari should dress regularly with a belt of straw and with deerskin garments. He should wear matted hair, carry a rod and waterpot and be decorated with a sacred thread, as recommended in the sastras.
