54 kamam nayatu mam devah kim ardhenatmano hi me dinena jivata duhkham anena vidhurayusa SYNONYMS kamam--as He likes; nayatu--let Him take away; mam--me; devah--the Supreme Lord; kim--what use; ardhena--with half; atmanah--of the body; hi--indeed; me--my; dinena--poor; jivata--living; duhkham--in suffering; anena--this; vidhura-ayusa--having a lifetime full of affliction. TRANSLATION If unkind Providence takes away my wife, who is half my body, why should He not take me also? What is the use of my living with half of my body, bereaved by loss of my wife? What shall I gain in this way?
