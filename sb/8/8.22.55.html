8.22.55

Text

purana-samhitam divyam sankhya-yoga-kriyavatim satyavratasya rajarser atma-guhyam asesatah

Synonyms

purana--the subject matter explained in the Puranas, the old histories, especially the Matsya Purana; samhitam--the Vedic instructions contained in Brahma-samhita and other samhitas; divyam--all transcendental literatures; sankhya--the philosophical way of sankhya-yoga; yoga--the science of selfrealization or bhakti-yoga; kriyavatim--practically applied in life; satyavratasya--of King Satyavrata; rajarseh-- the great king and saint; atma-guhyam--all the mysteries of self-realization; asesatah--including all branches.

Translation

The Supreme Personality of Godhead thus explained to King Satyavrata the spiritual science known as sankhya-yoga, the science by which one distinguishes between matter and spirit [in other words, bhaktiyoga], along with the instructions contained in the Puranas [the old histories] and the samhitas. The Lord explained Himself in all these literatures.
