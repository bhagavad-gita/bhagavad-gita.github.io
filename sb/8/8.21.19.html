19 baddham viksya patim sadhvi tat-patni bhaya-vihvala pranjalih pranatopendram babhase 'van-mukhi nrpa SYNONYMS baddham--arrested; viksya--seeing; patim--her husband; sadhvi--the chaste woman; tat-patni--Bali Maharaja's wife; bhaya-vihvala--being very disturbed by fear; pranjalih--with folded hands; pranata-- having offered obeisances; upendram--unto Vamanadeva; babhase--addressed; avak-mukhi--with face downward; nrpa--O Maharaja Pariksit. TRANSLATION But Bali Maharaja's chaste wife, afraid and aggrieved at seeing her husband arrested, immediately offered obeisances to Lord Vamanadeva [Upendra]. She folded her hands and spoke as follows. PURPORT Although Lord Brahma was speaking, he had to stop for a while because Bali Maharaja's wife, Vindhyavali, who was very agitated and afraid, wanted to say something.
