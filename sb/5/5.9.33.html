33 apare vasudevasya devakyam yacito 'bhyagat ajas tvam asya ksemaya vadhaya ca sura-dvisam SYNONYMS apare--others; vasudevasya--of Vasudeva; devakyam--of Devaki; yacitah--being prayed for; abhyagat-- took birth; ajah--unborn; tvam--You are; asya--of him; ksemaya--for the good; vadhaya--for the purpose of killing; ca--and; sura-dvisam--of those who are envious of the demigods. TRANSLATION Others say that since both Vasudeva and Devaki prayed for You, You have taken Your birth as their son. Undoubtedly You are unborn, yet You take Your birth for their welfare and to kill those who are envious of the demigods. PURPORT It is also said that Vasudeva and Devaki, in their previous birth as Sutapa and Prsni, underwent a severe type of penance to get the Lord as their son, and as a result of such austerities the Lord appeared as their son. It is already declared in the Bhagavad-gita that the Lord appears for the welfare of all people of the world and to vanquish the asuras, or the materialistic atheists.
