40 visrjya tam ca papraccha vatsa kasmad dhi rodisi kena va te 'pakrtam ity uktah sa nyavedayat SYNONYMS visrjya--throwing aside; tam--that; ca--also; papraccha--asked; vatsa--my dear son; kasmat--what for; hi-- certainly; rodisi--crying; kena--by whom; va--otherwise; te--they; apakrtam--misbehaved; iti--thus; uktah-- being asked; sah--the boy; nyavedayat--informed of everything. TRANSLATION He threw the dead snake aside and asked his son why he was crying, whether anyone had done him harm. On hearing this, the son explained to him what had happened. PURPORT The father did not take the dead snake on his neck very seriously. He simply threw it away. Actually there was nothing seriously wrong in Maharaja Pariksit's act, but the foolish son took it very seriously, and being influenced by Kali he cursed the King and thus ended a chapter of happy history.
