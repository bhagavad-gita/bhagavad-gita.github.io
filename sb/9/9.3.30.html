9.3.30

Text

avartamane gandharve sthito 'labdha-ksanah ksanam tad-anta adyam anamya svabhiprayam nyavedayat

Synonyms

avartamane--because of being engaged; gandharve--in hearing songs from the Gandharvas; sthitah-- situated; alabdha-ksanah--there was no time to talk; ksanam--even a moment; tat-ante--when it ended; adyam--unto the original teacher of the universe (Lord Brahma); anamya--after offering obeisances; svaabhiprayam-- his own desire; nyavedayat--Kakudmi submitted.

Translation

When Kakudmi arrived there, Lord Brahma was engaged in hearing musical performances by the Gandharvas and had not a moment to talk with him. Therefore Kakudmi waited, and at the end of the musical performances he offered his obeisances to Lord Brahma and thus submitted his long-standing desire.
